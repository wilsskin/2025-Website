Prototyped a Mobile Finance Calculator to Increase Buyer Confidence at Dealerships for 45M+ Monthly Users

CarGurus is one of the largest online automotive marketplaces in the U.S. with 45M+ monthly mobile users.

For my senior capstone, I collaborated with the CarGurus product team and three fellow students to design a mobile feature for buyers at dealerships. As a UX designer, I created Figma wireframes for the mobile finance calculator and built our final prototype using Lovable.

PROBLEM

Buyers struggled with fees and rarely used the CG app on the lot

Less than 10% of users opened the CarGurus app while at dealerships. Meanwhile hidden fees and confusing paperwork made buyers stressed and unsure whether they were getting a fair deal.

Lack of information transparency

80% of buyers said hidden fees and unclear pricing caused major stress

Slow and difficult negotiation process

Confusing paperwork left only 9.7% of buyers confident they got the best deal

Outdated tools with no customization

Generic calculators and undetailed listings failed to show true out-the-door cost

GOAL

Empower buyers during negotiations without undermining dealers

How might we empower buyers with clear financing information and motivate them to use the CarGurus app at dealerships, while still supporting dealer relationships?

1

Provide transparent cost estimates

2

Simplify financing steps and paperwork

3

Build and maintain trust with buyers & dealers

SOLUTION

We created a new finance calculator to prepare buyers for negotiations

The final prototype gives buyers a clear breakdown of all costs, simplifies financing steps, and serves as a meaningful resource for negotiations. I led the design direction across nine concept iterations and built the final mobile prototype with Lovable.

Project under NDA, reach out to access prototype.

01 - Finance Info & Goal Setting

Input your personal finance info

Buyers begin by confirming or editing prefilled finance details and setting a target monthly payment. Personalized suggestions guide users toward meeting their goals.

  • Prefilled finance info from prequalification or manually entered
  • Set a monthly payment goal and receive instant suggestions to meet it
  • Experiment with different inputs to see how they affect total and monthly cost

02 - Cost Breakdown

Anticipate hidden costs and add-ons

As users scroll, the calculator transitions into a receipt-style breakdown of taxes, fees, and add-ons. Optional dealer incentives and trade-ins dynamically adjust total costs.

  • View every fee, add-on, and tax clearly listed in one organized view
  • Add trade-ins or discounts at the dealership to reflect real-time negotiations
  • Build trust with dealers and reduce stress during the buying process

03 - Estimate Summary

Feel confident on-the-lot

The final section mirrors the financing calculations from a buyer’s order form, giving users a realistic estimate before entering negotiations.

  • Save and share estimates with clear total cost and payment
  • Compare saved estimates to explore different financing options
  • Reduce decision fatigue and arrive to the dealership with confidence

PROCESS

Research

Users rarely used the CarGurus app on-the-lot and were frustrated with hidden costs

Internal research showed the app was seldom used at dealerships, while secondary research and 5 buyer interviews revealed struggles with hidden fees and unclear financing terms. These findings highlighted cost and information transparency as a gap to address.

“Between the taxes, fees, and dealer add-ons, I had no idea what the real price was. I wish there was a way to see it all clearly without having to ask a million questions”

– Car Buyer Interviewee

Onsite research revealed that confusing paperwork overwhelmed buyers

We visited 3 dealerships, interviewed 5 dealers, and observed buyers struggling with financing paperwork. Buyer's order sheets scattered key information across multiple boxes, leaving buyers unsure if they were getting a fair deal.

Information transparency became the clear problem to solve

After consolidating 9 buyer interviews, 5 dealer interviews, and multiple on-the-lot observations, we mapped the entire car buying process. Buyers often lost confidence at each step, eroding trust in dealers and leading to stressful decision-making.

Prototypes

We explored 9 concepts to uncover the most promising directions

Our team brainstormed and sketched 9 potential solutions, from feature comparison tools to AR visualization. This exploration allowed us to consider a full range of potential directions before committing to one solution.

Three concepts showed the most promise

By informally testing with buyers and pitching to CarGurus, we discovered which concepts resonated with users. We mapped our findings onto an impact vs effort analysis and selected 3 concepts to build out in Figma.

1. QR Codes

Generate and scan QR codes to share info and save cars

2. Finance Calculator

Estimate out-the-door price with full cost breakdown

3. Deal-O-Meter

Show vehicle price range by area with good/fair/bad rating

4. Live Car Availability

Live updates on vehicle price and availability

5. Feature Comparison

Rank and compare feature packages to preferences

6. Appointment Booking

Book dealer visits and share financial info upfront

7. Car Buying Vocab

Tap on key terms for simple popup explanations

8. Car Comparing

View cars side-by-side to compare features and price

9. AR Visualization

Visualize cars and add-ons in real spaces

User testing showed the Finance Calculator was most effective

I built low-fi interactive flows in Figma and tested them with 6 participants. Feedback showed the Finance Calculator gave buyers the greatest clarity and confidence, so we chose it as the concept to refine further.

Design

New iterations added key features that addressed user needs

I led the design for the Finance Calculator, frequently testing with participants and refining the visual design to address user needs. Through feedback, I added features like a sticky finance banner, a monthly payment goal, and options to save or share estimates.

I brought the Finance Calculator to life with a Lovable prototype

Our Figma prototype conveyed the core flow, but complex interactions and live calculations weren’t possible. At CarGurus’ suggestion, I built the final prototype in Lovable, which brought the design to life with real functionality and gave me hands-on experience with AI-assisted design tools.
